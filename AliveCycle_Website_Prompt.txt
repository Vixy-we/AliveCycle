PROJECT OVERVIEW
----------------
Build a single-page marketing website for AliveCycle — a decentralized
environmental infrastructure service that converts greywater and biodegradable
waste into reusable water and nutrients through locally deployed, closed-loop
systems. The site's goal is to educate potential customers (municipalities,
institutions, residential societies, government bodies) about the problem and
AliveCycle's solution, and to drive service inquiries.


================================================================================
AESTHETIC DIRECTION
================================================================================

Tone:
  Organic-meets-technical. Not a generic "eco startup." Think: dark earth tones
  with precise engineering diagrams. Like if a soil scientist and an industrial
  designer built a brand together.

Color Palette:
  - Background:         Deep forest green #0D2B1F or rich dark soil #1A1208
  - Primary accent:     Warm bioluminescent green #7FD47A
  - Secondary accent:   Amber/ochre #C8922A (biofuel, nutrients)
  - Water accent:       Muted teal #4DB8B0
  - Text:               Off-white #F5F0E8

Typography:
  - Headlines:  A distinctive slab or high-contrast serif
                (e.g., Playfair Display, Fraunces, or DM Serif Display)
                — communicates weight, trust, and nature
  - Body:       Clean humanist sans
                (e.g., Lora, Source Serif, or Literata)
  - Labels/Tags: Mono font (e.g., JetBrains Mono) for data/metrics
                 — signals precision

Motion:
  Subtle scroll-triggered reveals. Flow animations on the system diagram
  (water flowing through pipes, leaves falling into composters).
  No flashy gimmicks — deliberate and calm.

Layout:
  Asymmetric sections. Data callouts breaking into margins. One diagonal
  divider between the problem and solution sections. The system diagram
  should be wide, horizontal, and visual.

Design DON'Ts:
  - No white background with green accents (too generic eco-startup)
  - No stock photo nature imagery
  - No purple gradients
  - No Inter / Roboto fonts
  - No generic "leaf icon" logos
  - No carousel sliders

2. THE PROBLEM (WHY THIS EXISTS)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Section Title: "The Gap No One Talks About"

Layout: Two columns side by side.

Left — Water Problem:
  Greywater from bathrooms, laundry, and kitchens is low-contamination and
  reusable — but it gets mixed with blackwater in municipal drains. That mixing
  makes both streams harder and costlier to treat. Recoverable water is lost.
  Freshwater extraction increases. Groundwater declines.

Right — Waste Problem:
  Organic food waste — kitchen leftovers, preparation scraps, mess waste — is
  high in nutrients. But it's landfilled or left to rot, releasing methane and
  leaching into soil. Those nutrients never return to the ecosystem.

Callout Box (full width, amber accent):
  "The result: excess freshwater extraction, declining groundwater, poor
  sanitation, and lost nutrients — all from the same urban block, every
  single day."

Feature Cards (below diagram):

  💧 Water Recovery System
     Greywater is diverted before mixing, treated locally using filtration
     and biological processing, stored, and reused — for landscaping, urban
     greenery, or groundwater recharge. No large infrastructure required.

  🌱 Nutrient Recovery System
     Organic waste is separated, oil is extracted for biofuel, and the
     remaining matter is processed into bio-fertilizer. It goes back into
     local soil — or is sold to peri-urban farmers.
